Founding Story2

Backline was born after the high-profile suicides of musicians Jeff Austin and Neal Casal in 2019. Hilary Gleason assembled eight co-founders — an agent, lawyer, marketing specialist, website builder, branding expert, and others personally affected by mental health crises — and spearheaded a task force of 100+ music industry professionals. They launched mere months before COVID-19, which immediately quadrupled demand for their case management services.

Co-founders identified from public records: Hilary Gleason (ED), Hallie Lincoln (Director of Case Management), Joshua Knight (The Team), Matthew Wilson (Arnall, Golden, Gregory LLP), and Stephen Grybowski (Dayglo Presents) — the latter three now serve on the board.3

Service Lines5

1. Case Management (Core Service)

The flagship offering: individuals contact Backline, complete a phone intake assessment, and receive a personalized care plan with curated referrals to therapists, psychiatrists, recovery coaches, and life coaches in Backline's vetted provider network.

2. B-LINE 24/7 Crisis Line (Launched January 27, 2026)6

A 24/7 hotline staffed by trained Vibrant Emotional Health counselors, bridging immediate crisis support with seamless handoff to case management. This expansion dramatically lowers the barrier to entry for musicians in acute distress and positions Backline as the comprehensive mental health touchpoint for the music industry.

3. Support Groups & Community

Green Room (peer support monthly meetings for musicians with lived mental health experience) and Real Talks (educational panels and conversations with artists). These foster peer connection and reduce stigma.

4. Global Resource Hub (Launched June 2025)7

An international directory of 111+ music-focused mental health organizations, created with Spotify's Heart & Soul initiative — Backline's first international expansion, establishing it as a knowledge broker. Contact: global@backline.care

5. Care on Tour & Wellness Resources

Tour support services and a wellness resource library round out the offering. Note: case management itself is US-only; the Global Resource Hub is the international on-ramp.

Growth Phases16

Phase 1
Volunteer Bootstrap (2019–2021)
Rapid response to urgent need after high-profile musician suicides. Co-founders donated time, expertise, and initial funding. Demand shock from COVID-19 validated the model. Growth organic, zero paid fundraising. Revenue: $220K → $363K (65% YoY).
Phase 2
Professionalization (2022–2023)
Transition from volunteer-led to staff-driven operations: first paid salaries appear in the 2022 990 (ED $52,590; wages $183,771). Provider network grew to 1,257+. Secured major board members (Spotify, Goldman Sachs). Revenue: $363K → $874K (63% + 48% YoY).
Phase 3
Institutional Scale (2024–present)
Service expansion (Global Hub, B-LINE crisis line, tour support) and artist-led fundraising (We Got You!). Established as US music industry standard for mental health — first nonprofit to win a Pollstar tour services award. Revenue: $874K → ~$980K (~13% YoY, BMF). Focus shifts from rapid growth to depth and sustainability.

Open Questions & Gaps

Data Gaps

Backline's full 2024 IRS 990 was not yet available via the ProPublica API as of June 11, 2026; the 2024 revenue figure comes from the BMF (Business Master File) extract, and the functional expense breakdown awaits the full filing. Donor-type composition (individual vs. foundation vs. corporate) is not disclosed in any 990. Provider network composition (therapists vs. psychiatrists vs. coaches) is not published. Geographic distribution of case management and crisis line volume is not disclosed. Long-term impact metrics (client retention, outcome measures) are not available in public filings — though the MusiCares × Amber Health tour study may begin to fill the outcomes gap industry-wide.

Strategic Questions for Taiwan Adaptation

How would Backline's model transfer to Taiwan's healthcare context (where mental health is still heavily medicalized and stigmatized)? Can an all-volunteer founding core work in Taiwan's employment culture? What funding mechanisms exist for mental health nonprofits in Taiwan (government grants, corporate CSR, individual donations)? Are there existing music industry organizations or unions that could serve as partners or distribution channels? How should the service be linguistically and culturally adapted (case management protocols, crisis line training, provider network recruitment)? What legal framework governs 501(c)(3)-equivalent organizations in Taiwan (財團法人 vs. 社團法人)?
