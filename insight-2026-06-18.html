01

Knowledge & Findings

The cycle produced a great deal — and sorting it by track is the finding.

The opposite of the silent month — but lopsided

May 1 – June 1 was "the silent month": zero articles, zero reports, zero new sources.1 June 1 – June 18 is loud. The wiki grew from 20 to 22 articles,2 two papers were read critically, the §5 citation system was built and retrofitted site-wide,3 PROJECT_RULES.md was restructured and cut ~14%,6 a Case Study hub shipped, news was demoted to a changelog, and the "Confidence → Basis" relabel landed.7 The machine ran at high volume.

Sort that output by track and the finding appears. The substantive items fall into two buckets — academic-track reading and project infrastructure — and the NPO/Taiwan track produced nothing: no primary data, no conversation on record, no MTAT registration, no funder or platform outreach.9 The volume is real; the allocation is the story.

What genuinely entered the evidence base

Two occupational-health papers were read. Rousseau et al. 2026 — the first scoping review of metal and rock musicians' physical and mental health, 47 studies across six domains — is now a complete report: critical reading, deploy page, wiki article, and a card, born citation-compliant and quality-passed.4 Its findings are genre-tinted risks rather than firm rates — hearing loss ~37.8% in one survey, headbanging injury modelled past ~75° of neck movement, a counter-intuitive "vocal resilience" — built on a case-report-heavy base: the burden is large and the evidence is immature.4 The second, Cerdá-Díaz 2026 (a musculoskeletal umbrella review), was drafted as Markdown and named the format reference implementation — yet it has no wiki article, no deploy page, no card. It sits orphaned mid-pipeline, flagged on June 12 and still open.5

Both deepen the occupational-health case, which serves the academic track. Rousseau's own limitations are the tell — nothing Asia- or Taiwan-specific, nothing on care access or the music economy, barely anything on social or financial well-being, "precisely where an NPO service-model angle would live." Even the new research points back at the gap.

One more piece of NPO-adjacent output landed on June 18, from the monthly org-study scheduled task: a third competitor deep-dive — Music Minds Matter / Help Musicians (UK) — born citation-compliant with a full design review.11 It is good work — but it is a third competitor case study, and June 1 was explicit that "Backline + MusiCares is sufficient" and more case studies are "forms of avoidance."1 The schedule produced the surplus item because that is what it is set to do. It belongs in the comfortable quadrant, not the Taiwan-signal column.

What the infrastructure bought

The citation system is not busywork. It binds claims to sources at the data level so fabrication becomes machine-detectable; the gates now pass on three converged pages and the Rousseau page passed a quality spot-check.3 The rules restructure addresses the exact "42 lines per wiki article" bloat the Critical Auditor flagged in May — the file is now a task route, ~14% shorter.6 The "Basis" relabel fixes a real honesty bug — a reader-facing "Confidence" label that did not mean statistical confidence.7 These were worth doing. Section 2 asks only whether they were the right call this cycle.

What is unchanged — and still the whole case

The core advocacy material has not moved because it did not need to: Musgrave & Lamis (2025), US male musicians at 138.7 per 100,000; MusiCares' Wellness in Music Survey as the operational template; Soundcheck (Canada, n=1,216) as the methodology for a Taiwan-equivalent study.10 The June 5 wiki check re-confirmed the Asia-Pacific gap by web search — still no Taiwan music-industry mental-health NPO — and logged the one live field movement: UMG's May 13 expansion with Amber Health and Project Healthy Minds.2 The first-mover thesis holds. It has held, unused, for three insights.

Trajectory

The curve didn't flatten — it bent toward the comfortable quadrant.

Throughput is not the constraint. Aim is.

June 1 described a curve asymptoting toward "the only thing the project produces is its monthly report about itself."1 That call was wrong by half. The project can produce at volume — it just demonstrated it. What the volume reveals is direction.

Observed from the workspace and git record, not a precise metric — the one exact number is the bottom bar.

The avoidance changed costume

In May the avoidance wore silence — easy to see, hard to deny. In June it wears productivity, which is harder to see and more seductive: I shipped a citation system, restructured the rules, and read two papers — I was productive. All true. And the one thing three consecutive insights have asked for — a single Taiwan human on record — stayed at zero.9 Productivity is the more sophisticated form of the same pattern named in April and May.

The two tracks are diverging, not stalling together

June 1 said "neither track is moving."1 That is no longer accurate, and the correction matters. The academic track moved — two papers, a deeper occupational-health base, and a citation system that makes the whole evidence deck credible to an external reader. The NPO track did not. The tracks are not stalled in parallel; they are separating. On current allocation the project is becoming, in practice, an academic research project wearing NPO branding.

Capability is no longer a plausible excuse

The validation gap is unchanged in substance — no primary data, no Taiwan signal — but its meaning has changed. In May one could wonder whether the project had the capacity to execute at all. June settles it: a citation system was retrofitted across the site in roughly a week.3 The project can ship hard things fast. It simply does not point that capacity at the NPO track. "I didn't have time" is off the table.

The clock, and one design correction

Grad-school applications open in roughly three months (September–December 2026); the MTAT conference flagged as the one Taiwan event on the calendar is happening this month with no registration on record;9 2027 NPO incorporation still needs founding relationships that do not exist. One honesty note: June 1 reported the design system "stable, untouched."1 It was touched heavily this cycle — citation markers, the relabel, a nav fix, a new hub, now v6.2.7 Healthy churn, but the "built once, done" story broke; design remains an active sink.

03

Direction Reflections

The June 14 gate was answered by silence — so read the revealed preference, not a fourth menu.

The gate was stepped over on the way to more governance

June 1 offered three options — resume as planned, pivot to academic-only, hibernate explicitly — and asked for a one-line decision in planning/ by June 14.1 No decision file exists. What the workspace shows instead is that on June 13, the day before the deadline, the project produced another rules-update proposal.9 The gate did not just slip; it was stepped over.

So this section will not offer a fourth menu — the menu format already failed once. It reads what the allocation has revealed. By revealed preference the project has already chosen: academic deepening plus infrastructure, while keeping NPO-track branding and the NPO-track scheduled tasks that manufacture monthly guilt. The drift is itself a decision — an unspoken one.

What the evidence base does not need — proven, not asserted

June said the project did not need more citations, more rule revisions, more design polish, or more competitor case studies — that these are forms of avoidance.1 This cycle ran the experiment: it produced exactly those things, at volume, and the needle on the central question did not move.9 The claim is no longer a worry; it is a result.

What it needs is unchanged and now embarrassingly cheap

One Taiwan human on record — a name, a date, one paragraph in raw/notes/. The research deck is loaded: Rousseau, Cerdá-Díaz, Soundcheck, Musgrave & Lamis, the MusiCares template.10 The missing step has never been more evidence; it is putting the evidence already in hand in front of one real person. The gun is loaded and has not been fired.

The binary, tied to an event rather than a willpower date

Willpower dates have failed twice — May 31, then June 14. So the gate this cycle attaches to something that fires on its own: the next scheduled task is the news scan on June 24.8 Before it fires, one of two things should be true.

A
One irreversible act
Send one message to one real person in Taiwan's music scene, and file one line in raw/notes/ — regardless of reply. The smallest possible unit of NPO-track work, still missing.
B
An honest rename
If not, rename the project academic-first: NPO track explicitly paused, NPO-branded scheduled tasks paused with it — so the schedule stops manufacturing a two-track story one track never touches.

Either is a clean outcome. The only unclean outcome is the current one: keeping the two-track identity while one track stays at zero and the monthly synthesis absorbs the guilt.

Workflow & Collaboration

Five workflow personas, grounded in this cycle's data — three proposals, two deliberate non-proposals.

Workflow Optimizer
Organization
Lens: session cadence, allocation, throughput, context-switching

The cadence is not the problem and never was — the scheduled tasks fire perfectly. This month the problem is finally visible in the throughput data itself: sessions clustered in infrastructure, governance, design, and academic reading; NPO-track sessions numbered zero.9 High throughput, pointed almost entirely away from the one track with a deadline.

The June 1 proposal here ("Taiwan Action Day, day 7") was not implemented — no such task exists.8 Re-proposing it repeats the failure. A different lever: not adding an NPO task, but closing the channel the energy keeps draining down.

Proposal 1 of 3 — Infrastructure freeze (30 days)

No edits to PROJECT_RULES.md, design-system.html, or any deploy-page redesign for the next cycle. The constraint is mechanical and auditable: a git diff on ルール/ and design-system.html should show zero lines changed at the next insight. The infrastructure is demonstrably sufficient — freezing it removes the comfortable place for effort to drain into. Before/after target: this cycle's large infra footprint → next cycle's zero.

Research Methodologist
Academic
Lens: pipeline efficiency, health-check → action gap, intake discipline

The diagnostic system works; the response system still does not. Two findings prove it. First, Cerdá-Díaz 2026 is named the §10 format reference implementation yet sits orphaned — Markdown only, no wiki article, deploy page, or card — flagged June 12 and still open.5 Second, the same two uncompiled raw files (Bergson et al. 2023; the JWF Annual Report) have been flagged across three health checks since April — roughly ten weeks.2 New reading was added while the queue aged. Intake is outrunning digestion.

Proposal 2 of 3 — Drain before intake

The next research-track session reads nothing new until the backlog clears: finish the Cerdá-Díaz triple (wiki + deploy + card, born compliant) and compile the two raw files into wiki/. Rule: no new paper enters the reading queue until the queue is empty. A one-session fix that also lifts the wiki health score and retires findings that have outlived two auditors.

Strategic Mentor
Life Direction
Lens: energy allocation, phase transition (building → using), honest labeling

I will not tell you to decide by a date — that failed on May 31 and again on June 14, and on June 13 the strategic-choice deadline was answered by drafting another rules proposal.9 Sit with that: faced with "choose your direction," the reflex was to improve the governance around the work rather than do the work. There is no shame in it — building is satisfying, low-risk, and visible; one cold message to a Taiwanese venue or label is uncertain and ego-exposing, and the brain reliably prefers the former. But this month removed the last excuse. You shipped a citation system in a week.3 The capacity is there. It is pointed elsewhere.

So the honest reading is the uncomfortable one: three insights of zero NPO contact is not a pause, it is data. Calling this a two-track project while one track never moves is the dishonesty that costs the most — it turns every monthly synthesis into a reminder of a thing not done.

Proposal 3 of 3 — One irreversible act, or an honest rename

Before the June 24 scan fires: send one message to one real person in Taiwan's music scene and file one line in raw/notes/. If that has not happened by June 24, adopt Option B explicitly — academic-first, NPO track and its branded tasks paused — and stop scheduling the guilt. Both are respectable. Drift is not.
