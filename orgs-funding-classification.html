Funding-model & service-type classification — all 110 organizations

Supplementary data for the Global NPO Landscape report. Primary source: the Backline Global Resource Hub directory (public; full 13-page roster, scraped 2026-06-12). Service types are the directory's own tags. Funding models are the author's classification — this table is the audit trail so every call can be checked.

Method — how each funding model was assigned

The directory carries no funding field, so funding model is not in the source data. Each organization was classified by one of three routes, with confidence stated:

  • ✅ Verified (11 orgs): documentary evidence read directly — IRS Form 990s (Backline, MusiCares), published annual reports (Help Musicians/Music Minds Matter), or official/government pages confirming public funding (CNM = French public establishment; Pro Helvetia = federally funded Swiss Arts Council; Creative New Zealand = national arts agency; SAMHSA = US federal agency; Support Act = Office for the Arts). Self-supporting fellowships AA/NA verified against their published Traditions.
  • ⚠️ Inferred from organisational type (45 orgs): assigned from the organisation's name and legal form, not from its financials — e.g. a national arts council or "Music Export" body → government/public-sector; a "Therapist Directory" / "Psicólogos…" listing → private-pay referral (not a funded NPO); a union / trade association / collecting society → industry-institution; a named "Fund"/"Foundation" → philanthropy; a 12-step/recovery fellowship → self-supporting. The exact trigger is in the Basis column. These are type-definitional inferences and have not been checked against each org's accounts.
  • ❓ No public funding info (54 orgs): organisational type alone did not justify a model and no public financials were located. Recorded as unknown — not guessed.

Distribution: No public info 54 (49%) · Government/public-sector 16 (15%) · Philanthropy/charity 16 (15%) · Private-pay/therapist directory 11 (10%) · Industry-institution 8 (7%) · Peer fellowship 5 (5%).
