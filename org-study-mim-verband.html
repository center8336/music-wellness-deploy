MIM-VERBANDMental Health in Music

Org Study · Est. 2019

↓ Scroll

One model, in one line

The U.S. answer to musician mental health is a charity; Japan's is a union welfare floor; Germany's statutory answer is the Künstlersozialkasse that folds freelance musicians into social insurance. MiM is none of these. It is a small collective of music-literate psychologists who put a "Verband" name on a field-building function — they raise awareness, curate the German-language knowledge base, knit a nationwide referral network, and deliver low-threshold counseling, much of it through other people's funded pools. It is the clearest case in this series of the "room" (service) built thin, federated, and on a shoestring, sitting on top of Germany's statutory floor. The honest finding: the room here is mostly plumbing and a few practitioners — no membership, no clinic, no reserve, and, on the public record, not even a registered association.

01 — Profile

A "Verband" that may not be a Verein23

MiM presents as the "MiM-Verband — Verband zur Förderung der mentalen Gesundheit in der Musikbranche," the self-described "central contact point for everyone in the music and creative industries with an interest in mental health."1 But the legal substance does not match the label — and that gap is the single most important fact on this page.

Accuracy flag — legal form ⚠️

The site's Impressum names a private individual — "Michael Wecker, Diplom-Psychologe" (Berlin) — as operator, with no e.V. in any legal name, no Vereinsregister (VR) number, no registration court, no Vorstand, and no Gemeinnützigkeit statement — all of which a registered association must publish under § 5 DDG. Every page reads "© Lauter, Wecker." A Vereinsregister search via handelsregister.de and Northdata returns no MiM entry, although the same searches surface other Berlin music-industry associations with their VR numbers. Combined, this indicates MiM is most likely an informal practitioner collective, not a registered Verein — a "Verband" in branding, not in law. (Absence of a registry hit is not proof of non-registration; we label this ⚠️, not a refutation.)23

02 — The model

Organize the field, deliver thinly, broker the rest

MiM's own three-verb mission is the map: Sensibilisieren & Informieren (awareness + knowledge), Netzwerken (a Germany-wide network of experts, affected people and institutions), and Beraten (counseling, coaching, workshops, training).1 German press states the operating reality plainly: "Der MiM-Verband organisiert vor allem psychologische Einzel- und Teamberatung, engagiert sich in Vorträgen als Speaker*innen und bietet auch Workshops und Seminare."4 The structure is a four-part field-building loop with no clinic and no fund at its center.

The field-building loop — service at the edge, no clinic at the center
MOVE 01

Awareness

Talks, panels, and a curated German-language library so the field has somewhere to point. The lowest-cost, most reusable thing MiM does.7

MOVE 02

Network

A deutschlandweit web linking music-literate psychologists, scenes and institutions. The founders teach as guest lecturers at hdpk Berlin, BIMM Berlin, SAE Berlin, Music Pool Berlin and the Institut für Systemisches Arbeiten — seeding the network from inside music education.4

MOVE 03

Refer

A published "MiM-Liste" of counseling and outpatient-psychotherapy addresses, plus pointers to general services and peer initiatives (MITC, Help Musicians UK, Music Minds Matter, MusiCares).7

MOVE 04

Deliver (lightly)

Actual music-aware counseling by a small pool, priced low and often paid for by partners. Service that scales with practitioner hours — the opposite of the awareness layer.5

The thing that makes this transferable is the same thing that makes it fragile: almost none of it requires scale or capital. No building to staff, no endowment to raise, no membership to administer. That is also why it is exposed — the "Verband" is, in substance, a handful of practitioners and a well-kept link list.

03 — What it delivers

Music-literate counseling, priced low, brokered wide5

The core deliverable is one-to-one (and team) psychological counseling by video call or phone, Germany-wide, from a pool of ~5 psychologists who are also musicians — trumpet/jazz, tour/studio, voice — so the counselor "understands your passion, your path and your environment." Self-pay pricing is explicit and modest.

Subsidized counseling runs on partners' money, not MiM's. For residents of Berlin, Hamburg, Bayern (München), Bremen and Mannheim, the same counseling is available at a low co-pay — but the subsidy comes from regional partner programmes: MusicPool Berlin, RockCity Hamburg, Feierwerk Fachstelle Pop (Munich, funded by Kreativ München), Pop Office Bremen, and Next Mannheim's "Creative Care." MiM supplies the music-literate practitioner; the partner supplies the funded slot. A standing "solidarity" action also offers pay-what-you-can sessions (€25 / €40 / €55).5

  • Core

    Psychological counseling

    Individual and team sessions, video/phone, Germany-wide. Themes: stage fright, burnout/stress, addiction, tour health, depression, self-management, handling public criticism, sexism/diversity, team conflict. The counselor is matched from the pool by topic.5

  • Workshops

    Sessions, workshops & panels

    Named formats include "Resilient durch die Krise," "Mental Health in der Musikbranche," "Stress lass nach," and "Homeoffice ohne Dachschaden," plus panel and keynote inputs across the industry.1

  • New · 2026

    The PRO MUSIK cooperation

    From 2026, MiM runs regular counseling with PRO MUSIK – Verband freier Musikschaffender e.V.: one-to-one counseling free to PRO MUSIK members, plus an open moderated group format ("Resonanzraum") ~every six weeks, in German and English. The model's natural growth path — plugging the practitioner pool into a real membership trade body that has the dues, the members, and the legal form MiM lacks.8

04 — Advocacy & field-building

Infrastructure for a field, not a caseload7

MiM's larger footprint is infrastructure for a field, not a caseload. Its "Wissenswertes" pages are a deliberately curated German-language clearinghouse: a downloadable directory of counseling/therapy addresses; the federal psychotherapy chamber's "Wege zur Psychotherapie" brochure (DE/EN/TR); general digital-care routes (MindDoc, HelloBetter); peer organizations at home and abroad (MITC, Help Musicians UK, Music Minds Matter, MusiCares); and a reading list of the field's key surveys (Record Union's 73 %, Help Musicians' Can Music Make You Sick?, the Tour Health Research Initiative).7 The stated long-term goal is cultural: that "mentale Gesundheit im Denken und Handeln der Musikbranche fest verankert ist."1

In this project's terms, this is room-building by curation and convening — making the existing care landscape navigable and music-aware, rather than standing up new clinical capacity. It is the German analogue to MusiCares' wellness survey or the Musicians' Union of Japan's published benefit schedule: the cheapest, most reusable asset an organization can leave behind.

05 — Strengths

What works

Music literacy as the product

Every counselor is also a musician; the whole pitch is "someone who understands the scene." For a population that distrusts generic services, who delivers the care is the differentiator — and it costs nothing but recruitment.

Capital-light and fast

No clinic, no endowment, no staff to speak of — three psychologists stood it up in 2019/2020. The whole model is reproducible by a small founding team.

Federated funding through partners

By plugging into MusicPool Berlin, RockCity, Feierwerk, Pop Office Bremen, Next Mannheim and now PRO MUSIK, MiM gets subsidized reach without raising the subsidy itself.

A field-building asset, not just a service

The curated knowledge base and referral list are the lowest-cost, most reusable thing it does — and exactly what a new market most lacks.

06 — Weaknesses & risks

What to watch

Legal-form opacity

Calling itself a "Verband" while showing no Vereinsregister entry and a private-person Impressum is a governance and trust risk — and a liability question for an org delivering psychological services.

Key-person fragility

A pool of ~5 (effectively led by 2), with the original third founder gone in 2023 and one lead "not taking new clients." There is no institutional body beneath the founders; the "Verband" is the people.

No independent funding

Revenue is fee-for-service plus other people's subsidies. No dues, no reserve, no published budget — capacity rises and falls with partner programmes MiM does not control.

Reach bounded by the pool

Subsidized counseling exists only where a regional partner funds it (five cities); elsewhere it is €50/€100 self-pay. Service that scales linearly with practitioner hours.

07 — Lessons for Taiwan

What a Taiwan music mental health NPO should take

  1. Lead with music-literate clinicians — the cheapest, highest-leverage move

    MiM's whole advantage is that its counselors are musicians. A Taiwan NPO can replicate this immediately: recruit a small pool of Mandarin-speaking, music-literate counselors and make "we understand the scene" the entire promise. No building required.5

  2. Build the referral map before the caseload

    The single most reusable thing MiM does is curate a navigable, music-aware list of who-helps-with-what. For Taiwan — where no such map exists — this is the first deliverable, and it doubles as evidence for funders.7

  3. Broker funded slots; don't try to fund them yourself

    MiM's subsidized counseling runs on partners' money (city pop offices, RockCity, PRO MUSIK). Look for the Taiwan analogue — a Ministry of Culture programme, a music-city office, a union — and supply the practitioner into their funded pool rather than raising the subsidy alone.8

  4. Decide your legal form on purpose — and state it

    MiM's "Verband"-without-a-Verein is a cautionary tale: the name implies a membership body and a governance structure it does not have, a trust and liability risk for clinical work. In Taiwan, choose deliberately between a 協會 and a 基金會, register it, and put the registration on the page from day one — "we are a real, accountable body" is part of the service.23

  5. Pair the room with the floor

    Germany has the strongest statutory floor in the cycle (the KSK folds freelance musicians into social insurance), and MiM deliberately builds only the music-aware care layer on top. Taiwan's lesson is the same from the opposite direction: don't try to be both the rights/welfare body and the service — partner with a labour/rights body (the 藝創工會 analogue) and own the care layer.9

  6. Grow by docking onto a real membership body

    MiM's 2026 PRO MUSIK cooperation shows the upgrade path: a thin practitioner collective becomes durable by plugging its pool into an established, dues-funded trade body. A Taiwan NPO can start thin and aim to become the standing mental-health service for an existing music membership organization.8
