Knowledge & Findings

What the research uncovered this cycle — including the finding that the cycle produced no new knowledge.

The cycle produced no new knowledge

The honest synthesis first: between May 1 and June 1, 2026, zero new wiki articles were authored, zero new reports were filed, and zero new raw sources were added to raw/. The only output of this cycle was the May 12 Rules Health Check — a scheduled audit of the rules file itself. The project produced governance about governance and nothing else.

This is not a small finding. It is the finding of the month, and Sections 1–3 must be read in that light. The "knowledge & findings" section is not generated in a vacuum — it reports on what changed. What changed this month is the absence of change.

What the evidence base still says (unchanged from May 1)

The mortality data from Musgrave & Lamis (2025) — US male musicians at 138.7 per 100,000 — remains the most consequential finding in the wiki. MusiCares' Wellness in Music Survey (11.4% suicidal ideation, >2× US national rate) remains the strongest operational template. Soundcheck (Canada, n=1,216) remains the methodological model for a Taiwan-equivalent survey.

None of this is new. All of it was true on May 1. The advocacy case has been ready to make for 31 days.

The Asia-Pacific silence is no longer just external

Last month the Asia-Pacific silence referred to the field — zero independent music MH NPOs across Taiwan, Korea, China, SE Asia. This month, the silence is also internal. The May 1 insight directed: "By May 31, have 5 informal conversations with people in Taiwan's music industry." The June 1 wiki and report folders contain no evidence that any conversation occurred. If conversations happened off-the-record, they did not produce notes, contacts, or themes filed into the project.

MTAT International Conference is happening this month (June 2026) — the only Taiwan event the wiki flagged as relevant. There is no indication of registration, attendance plan, or follow-up strategy. This is the single most concrete, time-bounded opportunity on the calendar.

What the rules health check found

The May 12 Rules Health Check ran on schedule and produced 18 findings (2 broken, 9 stale, 7 minor). Two findings worth carrying forward: (a) the naming-convention table is inconsistent and three actively-used report types — Insight, Academic_Biweekly, Critical_Reading — are unspecified; (b) PROJECT_RULES.md is 843 lines governing 20 wiki articles. The ratio of 42 lines of rules per wiki article was flagged by the Critical Auditor in May. It has not improved.

02

Trajectory

How the research is moving — the curve, the avoidance shape, and the cost of inaction.

The trajectory has flattened

A useful way to describe momentum: what is the curve of artifacts produced per month? April produced 7 reports plus the first monthly insight. May produced 1 report (the insight itself). June so far produces 1 report (this insight, again the insight itself). The curve is asymptoting toward "the only thing the project produces is its monthly report about itself."

This is the failure mode the Strategic Mentor flagged in April and the Critical Auditor flagged in May: infrastructure-building becomes the work. The infrastructure now runs autonomously — scheduled tasks fire on day 1, 5, 12 — and the absence of human input doesn't break the schedule, it just makes the schedule's output thinner.

The two-track question is no longer a question

The April insight framed two tracks: NPO startup (career, near-term) and academic research (grad school 2027). The May insight noted that "research so far has served the academic track primarily." June must be more direct: neither track is moving. No primary research has begun. No thesis question is locked. No advisor is contacted. No graduate program is applied to. The two-track problem hasn't been resolved; both tracks have stalled together.

The validation gap has widened, not closed

In May, the validation gap was: enough secondary research, no primary data. In June, the gap is the same — except the cost of inaction is now visible. Every month of delay means: one less month of survey runway before grad school applications (Sep–Dec 2026), one less month of network-building before any 2027 NPO incorporation, one less month before MTAT (June 2026) becomes a missed opportunity rather than a future one.

Spotify and platform partnerships remain unexplored

May noted Spotify as the emerging field accelerator (B-LINE, Heart & Soul, MusiCares × JED). No outreach was attempted to Spotify Taiwan or KKBOX in the intervening month. The hypothesis remains untested.

Direction Reflections

A decision among three options — and a gate to hold against the default of more silence.

The question this cycle must answer

The May insight closed with: "Who in Taiwan have you talked to?" The June answer, on the evidence in the workspace, is: nobody on record. June must therefore not pose a new question. It must hold the May question open and treat it as a yes/no gate.

Three honest options

The infrastructure is stable enough that the next month does not need more rules, more wiki articles, or more scheduled tasks. What it needs is a decision among three options:

01
Resume as planned
Execute the May proposals — five conversations, MTAT registration, one funder approach — within the next 30 days. Treat May 1–June 1 as a temporary pause.
02
Pivot to academic-only
Drop the NPO track explicitly. Use the existing evidence base as the foundation for a thesis proposal. The wiki is already sufficient for this. Begin advisor outreach.
03
Hibernate explicitly
Acknowledge that life circumstances make this month non-viable. Pause scheduled tasks. Set a return date. Avoid the cost of monthly insights that report on no work.

The worst option — and the one currently being executed by default — is to keep the schedule running while producing nothing. This generates a record of inaction that compounds psychologically. The next insight (July 1) will be even harder to write if the pattern continues.

The June 30 gate

If option 1 is chosen, a measurable gate by June 30 is: (a) MTAT conference attended OR registered with a stated plan, (b) at least 2 conversations on record with names, dates, and one-paragraph notes filed to raw/notes/, (c) one outreach email drafted (not necessarily sent) to Spotify Taiwan or KKBOX. If none of these three minimal deliverables is met by June 30, June 30 itself becomes the kill criterion for option 1.

What the evidence base no longer needs

It does not need: more competitor case studies (Backline + MusiCares is sufficient), more papers (the evidence is overdetermined), more rule revisions (Section 9 of PROJECT_RULES is the last addition that earned its keep), more design polish on existing pages. These are all forms of avoidance.

It does need: primary data, Taiwan-specific signal, real names of real people.

Workflow & Collaboration

Five workflow personas — three proposals, two deliberate non-proposals. Constrained by May's "max 3" rule.

Workflow Optimizer
Organization
Lens: session cadence, scheduling, throughput

The monthly cadence is operating exactly as designed. Day 1 produced this insight. Day 5 will produce a wiki health check. Day 12 will produce a rules health check. The schedule is not the problem. The problem is upstream of the schedule: there are no sessions feeding it. Between May 1 and June 1, the only "session" was the May 12 scheduled task running autonomously. The pipeline has perfect throughput on zero input.

Proposal 1 of 3

Add a single Taiwan Action Day on day 7 of each month with one task only — "Send one message to one person." Not a session block, not a research task, not a writing assignment. One outbound message to a real human in Taiwan's music industry. Success is the message sent, regardless of reply. This is the smallest possible unit of NPO-track work and it is currently missing entirely.

Research Methodologist
Academic
Lens: research pipeline efficiency, health check → action gap

The May health check found two uncompiled raw files persisting across two cycles (bergson-et-al-2023, JWF Annual Report 2025). The May insight proposed a "Fix Pass" the day after each health check. The June workspace contains no evidence of a Fix Pass having been added or executed. The proposal vanished into the same gap as the proposals it was meant to close.

The pipeline has a second-order problem: the diagnostic system (health checks) is healthy, but the response system is not. Auditors keep auditing, the audited never act, and the audit reports become the only artifact.

Proposal 2 of 3

Convert one of the four monthly scheduled tasks into a "Fix-Pass + Conversation Log" task instead. Pick one to retire. Methodologist's recommendation: retire the rules health check for the next two cycles (May 12 found the rules are stable enough). Replace it with a 60-minute block whose only artifact is raw/notes/conversations_YYYY-MM.md listing any human contact made that month. If empty, the file says so.

Strategic Mentor
Life Direction
Lens: pace sustainability, infrastructure vs. content ratio, decision gates

The pattern is unmistakable. April: 7 reports, 8 workflow proposals. May: 0 reports, 0.5 of 8 proposals executed. June: 0 reports, propose to re-propose. This is not a pace problem. This is an avoidance pattern — and naming it is what a mentor is for.

The avoidance has a shape: building governance is intellectually satisfying, low-risk, and produces visible artifacts. Sending one cold email to a Taiwanese venue owner is uncertain, ego-exposing, and may produce nothing. The brain reliably prefers the former. The schedule has been doing exactly what the brain wants — producing the comfortable artifact every month.

There is no shame in this. There is, however, a deadline. Grad school applications open in roughly four months. NPO 2027 incorporation requires partners who exist as relationships, not abstractions.

Proposal 3 of 3 — the only one that matters

Choose one of the three options from Section 3 by June 14. Not "by end of June" — by June 14. Two weeks. Write the choice down in planning/ as a one-line decision: "Option 1 / Option 2 / Option 3." Without that line, the July 1 insight will say the same thing this one says, only worse.
