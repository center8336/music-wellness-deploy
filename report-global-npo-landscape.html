Why it matters

What does the evidence say?

The need is real and measured. Canada's first national study (n=1,216) finds 86% of music professionals personally experienced mental-health challenges vs. ~12% of the general workforce; 43% have had lifetime suicidal thoughts vs. 12% in the general population.A

Prevalence findings across six studies · each row traced to its paperB
StudyCountryKey finding
Soundcheck / Harrison 2026Canada50–86% report MH challenges vs ~12% general workforce (n=1,216)
Musgrave & Lamis 2025UK/USMusicians among top-5 occupations for suicide; US male musicians 138.7/100K vs 14.2 general
Détari et al. 2020NorwayHigher demands, lower support, more work–family conflict vs general workforce (all p<0.001, n≈10K)
Vaag et al. 2016NorwayDepression/anxiety ~2× higher in musicians than general workforce
Kenny et al. 2014Australia23% depression, 33% social phobia among orchestral musicians
UK Musicians' CensusUK~30% of professional musicians report poor mental health
✅ Soundcheck final report (Revelios / Harrison, Feb 2026; n=1,216) + cross-study base. Primary PDF on file

What's moving in musician mental health?

Verified developments in musician and music-industry mental health, wellness and occupational health — abroad and in Taiwan — kept to items that change what a Taiwan music-MH NPO should know.

Updated biweekly · last published 2026-06-20

2026-05-14AbroadPrograms

Spotify & Backline release a songwriter mental-health guide — a free, copyable service template

A songwriter-specific guide built by Backline's clinical team under Spotify's Heart & Soul, debuted at a Nashville summit. The model — platform money, NPO delivery, one segment at a time — is the partnership a Taiwan org could pitch locally. → Source

Verified · Spotify Newsroom + MusicRow · Tier-1 press, two outlets
2025-10AbroadWorking conditions

Korea's Idol Labor Union puts mental-health protocols at the centre of its first demands

Union recognition filed Sep–Oct 2025 demands an enforceable agency MH-care manual, crisis protocols and cyberbullying legal support — but approval is contested and unresolved. East Asia's pathway runs through labor law, not philanthropy. → Source

Developing · Korea Times + Sports Kyunghyang · filing verified, status unresolved
2026-04-16AbroadResearch

The Global Wellness Institute names musician mental-health resources a top-4 wellness trend for 2026

An external wellness-economy body independently citing this field — B-LINE, Amber Health, MusiCares×JED — as a top trend is itself a datapoint: musician MH has entered mainstream wellness discourse, and the funding follows discourse. → Source

Verified · Global Wellness Institute · Tier-1 primary publication
