Key Findings

Backline's Global Resource Hub lists 111 unique organizations working in music-industry mental health and wellness (April 2026 baseline; June re-scrape: 110). Service types and funding models below are classified across all 110 (June full roster). Findings:

  • Two geographic poles — plus a borderless fifth. Among place-bound orgs the US leads (~21 standalone) and continental Europe is the second pole — Germany (~8), UK (~7), France (~6), Austria/Iceland/Switzerland/Romania 3 each, Australia 3. But ~20% of the directory (22 of 110) has no single home country — global helplines, 12-step fellowships and multi-country therapist networks. These are defined by service model, not geography; the place-bound majority cluster by national funding system. So for a fifth of the field, "what model" matters more than "where." 1
  • Zero organizations serve Taiwan — and zero independent, music-specific MH organizations exist anywhere in East or Southeast Asia. Japan's B-Side Project is corporate (Sony-contracted artists only); India's TATVA is the region's only independent music-specific org. 1
  • Service types are broader than clinical MH, and most orgs are narrow. Counted across all 110: Mental Health Support 44%, but Financial Resources (29%) and Career Development (28%) nearly rival it, then Counseling 21%, Substance Use 12%. Orgs are mostly single-purpose — mean 1.9 services each, 44% offer just one, only 25% combine three or more. The "successful orgs combine 2–3 services" pattern holds for the marquee names, not the long tail. 6
  • Funding is diverse — and for ~half the directory there is no public information. Classified across all 110 by organizational type: no public funding info ≈49% (shown as a fact, not guessed); government/public-sector ~15%; philanthropy/charity ~15% (Backline; Help Musicians/MMM 4); private-pay / therapist directory ~10%; trade body ~7%; peer fellowship ~5%. Beyond donations, a real government/public-sector cluster runs through European arts councils, ANZ and public health (CNM, Pro Helvetia, Creative NZ, Support Act 5, SAMHSA). 7 8 2
  • Taiwan mental-health policy baseline. The 全民心理健康韌性計畫 commits NT$5.63B over 2025–2030, including free counseling for ages 15–45. 3
